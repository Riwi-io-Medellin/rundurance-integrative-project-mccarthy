# RUNDURANCE — THE RACE PITCH
**Format: Immersive Running Experience | ~5–7 min presentation**

---

## 🏃 PRE-RACE — WARM UP
*[Ambient sound: runners stretching, shoes lacing, a crowd gathering at the starting line]*
*[Visual: dawn breaking over a track or road race]*

> **Narrator (low, calm voice):**
> "Every great race begins before the gun fires. With a ritual. With intention. With a coach who believed in you before you believed in yourself."

*Pause. Heartbeat sound fades in.*

> "Tonight, we're not giving you a presentation. We're taking you on a race."

---

## 🎬 MEET THE COACH — [VIDEO CUE]
*[Cut to video — 60 seconds]*
*Show the coach: early morning alarm, lacing up, heading to the track before sunrise. Voice over:*

> "His name is Carlos. He's been coaching endurance athletes for 11 years. Marathons, triathlons, ultras. He's helped 47 athletes cross finish lines they didn't think they could reach."
>
> "But lately, something changed. The races kept coming. The athletes kept growing. And Carlos... started drowning."
>
> *(Cut to: Carlos at his laptop at midnight, surrounded by Garmin exports, Excel sheets, WhatsApp messages)*
>
> "Not on the track. At his desk."

*Video ends. Silence.*

---

## 🔫 THE GUN FIRES — KM 0: THE HOOK
*[Sound: starting pistol. Crowd cheers. Pace picks up.]*

> **Speaker:**
> "Did you know that a high-performance coach spends **70% of their time analyzing data** on screens — and only **30%** actually coaching their athletes?"
>
> "We didn't build a software. We built a pacer. One that runs the data so Carlos can run with his people."

---

## 🏃 KM 1 — MEET YOUR PERSONA: EMPATHY MILE
*[Visuals slow down. Intimate camera. This is the emotional core.]*

> "Let us introduce you to **Mateo**."
>
> "Mateo is 34. Works in logistics. Wakes up at 5am three times a week to train for his first marathon. He pays Carlos $80/month because he trusts him with something personal — his body, his goal, his 42km dream."
>
> "But Mateo never knows if Carlos actually reviewed his last session. He uploads his Garmin data... and waits. Sometimes days. Sometimes he gets a generic message. Sometimes nothing."
>
> *(Beat.)*
>
> "Mateo doesn't quit. But he wonders — **does my coach really see me?**"
>
> "Carlos does. He just can't keep up."

*This is the pain. Let it breathe.*

---

## 🏃 KM 2 — THE PROBLEM: THE WALL
*[Visuals: runner hitting the wall, slowing down, grimacing]*

> "Right now, across every endurance academy in Latin America, coaches like Carlos face the same wall:"
>
> - Hours lost reviewing Garmin and Strava exports manually
> - Training feedback written one athlete at a time, from scratch
> - Payment follow-ups done through WhatsApp
> - No centralized view of who's improving, who's at risk, who's about to quit
>
> "This doesn't just burn out coaches. **It costs them athletes.** And it costs athletes like Mateo the experience they deserve."

---

## 🏃 KM 3 — THE SOLUTION: FINDING YOUR PACE
*[Visuals: runner settles into rhythm, breathing controlled, form perfect]*

> "That's why we built **Rundurance**."
>
> "An all-in-one platform powered by AI where the coach trains the system — once — with their own parameters, their own criteria, their own coaching voice."
>
> "And then the AI reads every session the way *they* would."
>
> "Mateo uploads his .FIT file from Sunday's long run. By Monday morning, he has a personalized feedback — written in Carlos's voice — waiting in his dashboard. Carlos? He approved it in 30 seconds."

---

## 🏃 KM 4 — KEY FEATURES: THE FUEL STATIONS
*[Each feature is a water station — relief, energy, momentum]*

**Station 1 — Trainable AI Agents**
> "Carlos teaches the AI his rules. Z2 heart rate for base building. Power-to-pace ratio for tempo. The AI learns. Carlos scales."

**Station 2 — Integrated Financial Management**
> "No more chasing payments on WhatsApp. Rundurance tracks every subscription, flags overdue payments, and keeps Carlos's business as sharp as his training plans."

**Station 3 — The Athlete Portal**
> "Mateo opens his phone. He sees every session. Every PR. Every week of progress stacked into a visual timeline. He doesn't just feel trained — he feels **seen**."

---

## 🏃 KM 5 — TECH STACK: THE INFRASTRUCTURE
*[Visuals: race infrastructure — timing chips, pacers, water stations, medical support]*

> "A race this big needs serious infrastructure."
>
> - **Backend:** Node.js + Express — fast, scalable, real-time
> - **AI Orchestration:** n8n — intelligent pipelines between wearables and AI agents
> - **Frontend:** HTML5 + Tailwind CSS — coach dashboard and athlete portal, any device
> - **Storage:** AWS S3 — every .FIT file, every session, secured and retrievable
>
> "We chose this stack because it lets us move from prototype to production at race pace — without sacrificing the reliability that biometric data demands."

---

## 🏃 KM 6 — COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE: PULLING AWAY FROM THE PACK
*[Visuals: runner surges, gap opens with the field]*

> "Strava shows you the data. TrainingPeaks shows you the charts."
>
> "Rundurance **reads them for you.**"
>
> "Our edge is cognitive automation — the AI doesn't just store information, it interprets it through the coach's eye. This is not a dashboard. This is a second brain."

---

## 🏃 KM 7 — FUTURE VISION: THE HORIZON
*[Visuals: open road, no finish line in sight — limitless]*

> "We're just getting started."
>
> - **Wellness Integration:** Connect Apple Health and Google Fit to cross-reference HRV, sleep, and stress — so the AI flags burnout *before* it happens
> - **Smart Plan Builder:** Coach sets the goal — "Sub 3:30 marathon" — AI drafts the 12-week plan based on the athlete's actual data
> - **Profitability Dashboard:** Carlos discovers which plans retain athletes longest, and which profiles grow his revenue — turning passion into a data-driven enterprise

---

## 🏁 FINISH LINE — THE CLOSE
*[Sound: crowd roaring, finish line tape, medal placed around a neck]*
*[Visuals: Mateo crossing his finish line. Carlos watching from the side, arms raised.]*

> "Mateo crossed the finish line at 3:41:07."
>
> "It wasn't Carlos's data that got him there. It was Carlos's coaching."
>
> "We just gave Carlos his time back."
>
> *(Beat. Look at the audience.)*
>
> "We're not here to sell management software. We're here to eliminate coach burnout — so that every Mateo finds a Carlos who has the energy to actually show up."
>
> "It's time to let technology do the heavy lifting. And let the coach get back to being a coach."
>
> **"Welcome to Rundurance. Let's race."**
